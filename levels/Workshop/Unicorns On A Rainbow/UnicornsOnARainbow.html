Unicorns on a Rainbow


You are going to make a unicorn follow the mouse.

1. Create a new sketch in Processing and add setup() and draw() methods.

2. Find a photo of a rainbow:

  • Find an image on google
  • Right click on the image and select "Save Image As"
  • Rename your image something short (e.g. "rainbow")
  • Save the image on your desktop
  • Drag the image from your desktop and drop it into your sketch


3. On line 1 of your sketch, add the following code:

PImage rainbow;
PImage unicorn;

4. Load your image by adding the following code to your setup() method:

rainbow = loadImage("rainbow.jpg");
size(800, 600);
rainbow.resize(width,height);

5. If needed, change “rainbow.jpg” from the previous step so that it matches the name of your image.

6. At the beginning of your draw() method, set rainbow as your background using the background() method.

7. Find an image of a unicorn on google. Make sure it has a transparent background. Search for “unicorn transparent” or “unicorn png”. Repeat the instructions in step 2 to add it to your program.

8. In your setup()method, load your unicorn image using the following code:

 unicorn = loadImage("unicorn.png");

9. If needed, change “unicorn.png” from the previous step so that it matches the name of your image.

10. In your draw() method, add the unicorn using:

image(unicorn, 400, 300);

11. Change the previous line of code so that the unicorn moves with your mouse (HINT: use mouseX and mouseY).

12. Inside of your draw statement, redraw the background just like you did in the setup() method. This will make it so that only one unicorn appears at a time.
